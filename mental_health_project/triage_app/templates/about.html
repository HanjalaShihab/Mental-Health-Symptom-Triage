About Mental Health Symptom Triage

What is This Tool?

The Mental Health Symptom Triage tool is a digital screening application that helps individuals assess their mental health symptoms based on established clinical guidelines.

It uses the WHO mhGAP (Mental Health Gap Action Programme) framework to categorize symptoms and provide preliminary assessments.

How Does It Work?
  1. Assessment: You answer 20 carefully selected questions about your mental health symptoms
  2. Analysis: The system analyzes your responses using clinical guidelines
  3. Results: You receive a severity classification and personalized recommendations
  4. Resources: The tool provides guidance on next steps and helpful resources
Severity Levels
✅ No Significant Concerns

Minimal or no symptoms detected. Maintain healthy routines.

🟢 Mild Concerns

Some symptoms present. Consider counseling if they persist.

🟡 Moderate Concerns

Multiple symptoms affecting daily life. Professional help recommended.

🔴 Severe Concerns

Significant symptoms. Urgent professional help needed.

🚨 Immediate Crisis

Acute risk. Emergency services needed immediately.

⚠️ Important Disclaimer
  • This tool provides preliminary screening only
  • It is NOT a diagnosis or substitute for professional assessment
  • Cannot replace care from qualified mental health professionals
  • Results should be discussed with a healthcare provider
  • If in crisis, seek immediate professional help
Clinical Framework

This tool is based on the WHO mhGAP guidelines, which provide evidence-based recommendations for the assessment and management of mental, neurological, and substance use disorders.

The questions cover symptoms associated with:

  • Depression and mood disorders
  • Anxiety disorders and panic
  • Psychotic symptoms
  • Risk of self-harm
  • Substance use patterns
  • Cognitive and behavioral changes
Privacy & Data

Your assessment data is stored locally and used only to provide you with personalized results and recommendations. We do not share your information with third parties.

Get Help

If you're in crisis or experiencing thoughts of self-harm:

  • 📞 Emergency Services: Call 999 (UK) or 112 (EU)
  • 💬 Crisis Text Line: Text to a designated crisis line
  • 🏥 Emergency Room: Go to your nearest hospital
  • 👨‍⚕️ Talk to Someone: Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or professional
